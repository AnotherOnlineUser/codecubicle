Popular Destinations

Red Fort (Lal Qila)

Location: Old Delhi

Description: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this iconic fort is a symbol of India's rich history and Mughal architecture.

India Gate

Location: Central Delhi

Description: A war memorial dedicated to Indian soldiers, this grand archway is a prominent landmark in the heart of the city.

Qutub Minar

Location:Mehrauli

Description: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, this towering minaret is one of the tallest in the world, made of red sandstone and marble.

Humayun's Tomb

Location:Nizamuddin East

Description: The tomb of Mughal Emperor Humayun, this structure is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and a precursor to the Taj Mahal.

Lotus Temple

Location:Kalkaji

Description: A Bahá'í House of Worship, the Lotus Temple is famous for its flower-like architecture and serene ambiance.

Jama Masjid

Location: Old Delhi

Description: One of the largest mosques in India, this stunning structure is an important religious site and a prime example of Mughal architecture.

Akshardham Temple

Location:Pandav Nagar

Description: A modern Hindu temple complex, Akshardham is known for its grand architecture, spiritual exhibits, and light and water shows.

Lodhi Gardens

Location:Lodhi Road

Description: A serene oasis in the heart of the city, Lodhi Gardens is a historical park dotted with 15th-century tombs, lush greenery, and walking paths.

Dining

Street Food Experience

Paranthe Wali Gali

Location: Chandni Chowk

Description: Famous for its variety of stuffed paranthas (Indian flatbreads), this narrow lane in Old Delhi is a must-visit for food enthusiasts looking to savor authentic, rich flavors.

Karim’s

Location: Jama Masjid

Description: Established in 1913, Karim’s is an iconic spot known for its Mughlai cuisine, especially the tender kebabs and mutton dishes, offering a taste of Delhi’s royal past.

Khan Chacha

Location: Khan Market

Description: A legendary name for North Indian street food, Khan Chacha is famous for its delicious rolls, kebabs, and tikkas, with a legacy spanning several decades.

Paharganj

Location: Chandni Chowk

Description: Renowned for its chole bhature, this humble eatery is a favorite among locals and tourists alike, serving some of the best North Indian street food in Delhi.

Fine Dining Experience

Bukhara

Location: ITC Maurya, Diplomatic Enclave

Description: An internationally acclaimed restaurant, Bukhara is famous for its rustic North-West Frontier cuisine, including the legendary dal Bukhara and the massive tandoori Naan.

Indian Accent

Location: The Lodhi, Lodhi Road

Description: Indian Accent offers an inventive take on traditional Indian dishes, blending global flavors with local ingredients. It’s one of India’s top fine dining restaurants, known for its innovative menu.

Olive Bar & Kitchen

Location: Mehrauli

Description: A beautiful Mediterranean restaurant set in a restored colonial building, Olive Bar & Kitchen offers a romantic atmosphere with gourmet European dishes and fine wines.

The Spice Route

Location: The Imperial, Janpath

Description: An opulent dining experience that takes you on a culinary journey through the spice route of Asia, from India to Thailand, in a setting adorned with intricate artwork and antiques.
